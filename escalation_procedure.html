Escalation Procedure

Understanding how we handle escalations in our support structure.

What is an Escalation Procedure?

The escalation procedure is a structured process we use to address issues that require additional support beyond the initial point of contact. This ensures that every issue is handled by the right team or individual with the necessary expertise, allowing us to resolve problems effectively and efficiently.

Call Center Escalation Flow: Hierarchical and Functional models in large organizations.

Hierarchical Escalation

In a hierarchical escalation model, issues are escalated through a chain of command. The escalation flows upward until it reaches a team member with the authority or knowledge required to handle it. This model is useful for situations where issues need higher-level approvals or expertise. The hierarchy typically includes the following roles:

  • Tier 1 Agent: First point of contact for basic support and initial troubleshooting.
  • Team Lead: Oversees Tier 1 agents and can provide additional assistance if needed.
  • Supervisor: Addresses more complex issues or cases that require oversight.
  • Manager: Involved in cases that need management intervention.
  • Department Head: Handles critical cases or escalations that require top-level attention.

Functional Escalation

The functional escalation model involves routing issues to specialists or team members with the specific skills needed to address them, regardless of their position in the organization. This model is ideal for issues that require specialized knowledge rather than managerial oversight. The functional model generally includes:

  • Initial Contact Agent: First point of contact who attempts basic troubleshooting.
  • Specialized Department Member: A specialist in a specific area (e.g., software, hardware) who can handle more advanced issues.
  • Cross-Functional Team Members: Members from various departments working together to address complex, multi-faceted problems.
  • Executive: The highest level of escalation, generally for issues impacting the entire organization or requiring executive approval.
